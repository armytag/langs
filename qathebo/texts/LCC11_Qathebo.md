#### Vowel Assimilation

Vowels undergo a pervasive, regular assimilation process based on the following vowel.  The low vowel /a/ becomes \[É›\] when the following syllable contains /i/, and /i/ likewise assimilates to \[É›\] before a syllable with /a/.  The rounded vowel /o/ instead undergoes a dissimilation process with following /o/ syllables, also becoming \[É›\].  These patterns can be seen in the following examples.

#### Animacy Groups (Class/Gender)

There are five classes of nouns that determine the noun's declension pattern as far as number marking, verb and modifier agreement, and word order.

***Group 1*** -- Humans, including human body parts and groups of humans (e.g. society).

***Group 2*** -- Animate, self-moving things such as animals, waterfalls, fire, and animal body parts.

***Group 3*** -- Manipulatable objects such as cooked food, tools, small stones, and crops.

***Group 4*** -- Larger environmental objects such as trees, buildings, and clouds.

***Group 5*** -- Abstract, formless things such as thoughts, ideas, emotions, and time.

#### Person & Number

Nouns always mark number.  *Group 1* and *Group 2* differentiate single, dual, paucal, and plural.  In *Group 3* and *Group 4* the dual and paucal merge.  *Group 5* only distinguishes single and plural, and uses opposite marking to *Group 1*.  This is summarized in the table below.

|         | Single | Dual  | Paucal | Plural |
| ---:    | :---:  | :---: | :---:  | :---:  |
| Group 1 | -ta    | -thi  | -tha   | -gi    |
| Group 2 | -ma    | -tha  | -thi   | -mi    |
| Group 3 | -ga    | -ki   | -ki    | -la    |
| Group 4 | -qo    | -hlo  | -hlo   | -la    |
| Group 5 | -gi    | -ta   | -ta    | -ta    |
: Nominal Person-Number Marking

These number-marking suffixes only appear in nouns.  Regardless of the Animacy Group of the relevant noun, [verb agreement](#direct-object-agreement) and [modifier agreement](#modifying-nouns) only distinguish single and plural.

#### Possession

When nouns are possessed, they take an additional suffix *-tÊ¼o* after number and before the case marker.  This form of possession only applies to nouns of a lower animacy group than the possessor; something cannot be possessed by a noun of equal or lower animacy.  The possessor noun appears in genetive form after the possessed noun, much like an noun modifier.

##### Relative Tense

Within relative clauses the past and remote-past merge into a single tense, leaving three tense distinctions: **future** (*-ta*), **present** (*-na*), and **past** (*-pÊ¼i*).  The point of reference for these relative tenses is not the moment of speech, but rather the time of the event in the matrix predicate.  See the [Relative Clauses](#relative-clauses) section for examples.

### Demonstrative Pronouns

There are two demonstrative pronouns in QÊ¼athebÊ¼o.  One is for referents near the speaker, the proximal demonstrative *lothi*, and the other is for referents far from the speaker, the distal *kedi*.  These demonstratives take the same number marking as other nouns, matching the appropriate animacy group of the referent.
